The Greens & Hot Shot Division is comprised of a dedicated team of thirty (30) full-time employees, led by two (2) supervisors and one (1) forester. This team is responsible for the ground’s maintenance of city-owned and leased properties, street right-of-way, and drainage right-of-way.

Greens

The Greens branch is responsible for mowing, edging, trimming trees, watering trees, and emergency tree removals within city-owned/ leased property and right-of-way.

Key Achievements:

  • Evaluated and Trimmed Trees: 3,465 trees
  • Removed Damaged or Diseased Trees: 675 trees
  • Watered Trees: 10,772 trees
  • Mowed Sites in Brownsville: 3,568 sites
  • Planted or relocated 282 trees
  • Planted or Relocated Trees: 282 trees

Utility

The Greens Division is more unique than any other division within the Public Work Department. Their main responsibilities are to ensure that the City’s medians and easements are maintained. Some streets medians for example are; FM 802, Palm Boulevard, Morrison Road, University Boulevard, Mexico Boulevard, just to name a few. This division also takes pride in assisting other divisions and departments throughout the City. The division assists the street maintenance, the Fire Department, the Police Department, Health Department, the Parks Department and in multiple City sponsored events. This division is responsible for trimming the trees for the Streets division to ensure clearance for the paving equipment. This division is responsible for mowing, trimming and cleaning the Fire and Police Department training centers. During FY-2017, the division had dedicated two (2) full-time employees to assist the Health Department with the fight against the Zika Virus. These employees installed mosquito traps, check them and harvest mosquito to be analyzed.  

Hot Shot

The Hot Shot branch is responsible for mowing drainage right-of-way and street sweeping public streets. The maintenance activities include mowing 108,480 linear feet (approximately 20.5 miles or 200 acres) of drainage ditches, completing one cycle every 3 months, totaling 4 cycles per year, weather permitting. Street sweeping covers 960 linear miles throughout the city, with 6 cycles completed annually according to the department's work plan. Additional activities include addressing illegal dumping, Health Department reports, assisting with downed trees, curb scraping, daily downtown cleaning, and responding to emergency calls from the police department or city manager's office.

Key Achievements:

  • Mowed Ditches: 160 miles
  • Scraped Curbs: 39 miles
  • Swept Streets: 10,827 miles
