The City Forester is responsible for the management and maintenance of Brownsville’s urban forest by working to enhance the ecological, aesthetic, and social benefits of our urban trees while addressing challenges such as tree health, pest management, and urban development. The City Forester is responsible for leading innovative urban forestry initiatives that enhance green infrastructure, foster biodiversity, and improve community resilience through strategic planning and management. The City Forester collaborates frequently with community partners on tree-planting programs and public speaking events.

The City Forester ensures compliance with the Unified Development Code by processing tree removal applications, reviewing landscape plans, and conducting site inspections to ensure compliance with Brownsville’s Unified Development Code.

The City Forester works together with the Brownsville Beautification Committee and Keep Brownsville Beautiful as their city liaison, assisting them with beautification projects across the city. Recently, the BBC created an annual forestry fund of $50,000 for tree planting initiatives in each of the four municipal districts in alignment with their goal to achieve 30% tree canopy coverage city-wide. The City Forester is responsible for maintaining Brownsville’s Tree City USA and Scenic Texas certifications.

Our City Forester, Hunter Lohse, is a licensed landscape architect, an ISA Certified Arborist and Urban Forest Professional, is accredited by the U.S. Green Building Council (LEED AP ND), the Sustainable Sites Initiative (SITES AP), and the Institute for Sustainable Infrastructure (ENV SP).
